Traveling with Pets: Safety Tips and Best Practices

February 25, 2026 7 min read

Traveling with your pet can be a wonderful adventure, but it requires careful planning and preparation. Follow these expert tips to ensure a safe and enjoyable journey for both you and your furry companion.

Pre-Trip Preparation

Visit Your Veterinarian

Schedule a check-up 2-4 weeks before your trip:

  • Ensure vaccinations are current
  • Get a health certificate (required for air travel and crossing state lines)
  • Discuss motion sickness prevention if needed
  • Update ID tags and microchip information
  • Get prescription refills if traveling for extended periods

Research Your Destination

  • Confirm pet-friendly accommodations
  • Locate nearby veterinary clinics
  • Check local pet regulations and leash laws
  • Find pet-friendly activities and restaurants
  • Research local hazards (wildlife, plants, weather)

Travel by Car

Safety Equipment

  • Carrier or crate: Secure in backseat or cargo area
  • Harness and seatbelt: For dogs not in carriers
  • Pet barrier: Keeps pets in backseat area
  • Never let pets ride in the front seat or on your lap

Road Trip Tips

  • Take breaks every 2-3 hours for bathroom and exercise
  • Never leave pets alone in parked cars
  • Bring plenty of water and offer it frequently
  • Feed light meals or wait until destination to prevent car sickness
  • Keep windows partially open but not enough for escape
  • Pack favorite toys and blankets for comfort

Air Travel

Cabin vs. Cargo

In-Cabin Travel:

  • For small pets (usually under 15-20 lbs)
  • Carrier must fit under seat
  • Book early—limited spots available
  • Fees typically $75-200 each way

Cargo Hold:

  • For larger pets
  • IATA-approved crate required
  • Direct flights preferred
  • Avoid extreme weather conditions

Airline Preparation

  1. Confirm airline's pet policy weeks in advance
  2. Get health certificate within required timeframe (usually 10 days)
  3. Acclimate pet to carrier beforehand
  4. Attach ID tags to carrier with contact info
  5. Freeze water in bowl to prevent spills
  6. Exercise pet before flight

Packing Essentials

Food and Water

  • Bring enough regular food for entire trip plus extra
  • Portable food and water bowls
  • Bottled water (unfamiliar water can upset stomach)
  • Treats for rewards and comfort

Health and Safety

  • Current medications with prescription labels
  • First aid kit specific for pets
  • Vaccination records and health certificate
  • Your vet's contact information
  • Pet insurance card if applicable

Comfort Items

  • Favorite toys and blankets
  • Bed or sleeping pad
  • Leash and extra collar
  • Waste bags and cleaning supplies
  • Calming aids if recommended by vet

Accommodation Tips

Finding Pet-Friendly Lodging

  • Use pet-friendly booking sites
  • Call ahead to confirm pet policy
  • Ask about pet fees, size limits, and restrictions
  • Request ground floor rooms for easy access
  • Check if there are designated pet relief areas

Being a Courteous Guest

  • Never leave pets unattended in room
  • Keep pets off furniture unless allowed
  • Clean up after your pet immediately
  • Keep noise levels down
  • Report any damages promptly

International Travel

Additional Requirements

  • Research destination country's pet import regulations
  • Allow 6+ months for required preparations
  • Rabies vaccination and titer tests often required
  • Microchip may be mandatory
  • USDA-endorsed health certificate needed
  • Quarantine periods in some countries

Managing Travel Anxiety

Preparation Strategies

  • Practice short trips before long journey
  • Create positive associations with carrier
  • Maintain normal feeding/walking schedule
  • Exercise before travel to reduce energy
  • Use calming pheromone sprays
  • Consider anxiety medication for severe cases (vet prescribed only)

During Travel

  • Stay calm—pets sense your stress
  • Speak in soothing tones
  • Provide familiar items
  • Stick to routine as much as possible
  • Reward calm behavior

Safety at Your Destination

Exploring New Areas

  • Always use a leash, even if pet is well-trained
  • Check for local hazards (snakes, toxic plants, etc.)
  • Avoid areas with heavy wildlife
  • Keep pet away from unfamiliar animals
  • Watch for signs of overheating in warm weather
  • Ensure pet wears ID tags at all times

Emergency Preparedness

What to Do If Pet Goes Missing

  1. Contact local animal control and shelters immediately
  2. Post on local lost pet social media groups
  3. Create and distribute flyers with photo
  4. Check with veterinary clinics for microchip scans
  5. Use lost pet apps and websites
  6. Keep searching—many pets are found within days

Medical Emergencies

  • Have emergency vet contact info ready
  • Know location of nearest 24-hour animal hospital
  • Bring medical records on phone or printed
  • Pet insurance may cover emergency visits

Alternatives to Traveling with Pets

Sometimes leaving your pet at home is the better choice:

  • Professional pet sitting: In-home care in familiar environment
  • Boarding facilities: Quality facilities with trained staff
  • Trusted friends/family: Care from familiar people

Consider leaving pet behind if:

  • Trip involves extreme temperatures
  • Destination is not pet-friendly
  • Pet has severe anxiety or health issues
  • Schedule is too hectic for proper pet care

Final Thoughts

Traveling with pets requires extra planning, but the joy of having your furry friend by your side makes it worthwhile. With proper preparation, safety measures, and patience, you can create wonderful travel memories together.

Remember, every pet is different. Some love adventure while others prefer staying home. Know your pet's temperament and make the decision that's best for their well-being.
